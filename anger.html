The mood of anger is an emotional state characterized by feelings of hostility, irritation, frustration, or rage. It is often accompanied by physiological responses such as increased heart rate, rapid breathing, and muscle tension. Anger can be a normal and healthy response to certain situations, such as when we feel our rights have been violated or when we need to set boundaries. However, when anger is chronic or expressed in unhealthy ways, it can lead to negative consequences in personal relationships, work environments, and overall mental and physical health.

Anger is an intense emotional state characterized by strong feelings of annoyance, displeasure, and hostility, usually resulting from a perceived threat, injustice, or frustration. It is a powerful mood that can lead to various physical and psychological reactions, such as increased heart rate, high blood pressure, sweating, tense muscles, and aggressive behavior. Anger can also manifest in various degrees of severity, ranging from mild irritation to explosive rage. It is a normal and natural emotion, but when it becomes frequent, intense, or uncontrollable, it can negatively impact one's relationships, work performance, and overall well-being. Therefore, it is important to recognize and manage anger in a healthy way, such as through effective communication, relaxation techniques, and seeking professional help if necessary.
